Water, sanitation and
hygiene interventions
to prevent diarrhoea
Intervention | Last updated: 9 August 2023
Diarrhoea remains a leading cause of death globally among children under five years of
age. Diarrhoea contributes to nutritional deficiencies, reduced resistance to infections
and impaired growth and development. Severe diarrhoea leads to fluid loss, and may
be life-threatening, particularly in young children and people who are already
malnourished or have impaired immunity.
Diarrhoea mostly results from lack of safe drinking-water, adequate sanitation and
hygiene. A number of interventions are effective in preventing diarrhoeal diseases,
thereby positively impacting the nutritional status of those most vulnerable. These are:

[page 2]
Access to safe drinking water (e.g. water safety planning (the management of water
from the source to tap); household water treatment and safe storage)
Access to improved sanitation facilities
Hand washing with soap at critical times (e.g. after toilet use and before the
preparation of food)
Hygiene promotion, along with access to safe drinking water and adequate sanitation
should be accessible by all.
